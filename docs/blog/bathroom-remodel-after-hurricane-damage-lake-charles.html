Bathroom Remodel After Hurricane Damage in Lake Charles

Restoring and rebuilding bathrooms damaged by hurricanes and flooding across Calcasieu Parish.

Lake Charles and Calcasieu Parish have experienced some of the most devastating hurricane seasons in recent memory. Hurricane Laura made landfall in August 2020 as a powerful Category 4 storm, causing catastrophic damage to homes across Southwest Louisiana. Just weeks later, Hurricane Delta followed the same path, compounding the destruction. For many homeowners, bathrooms were among the hardest-hit rooms due to water intrusion through damaged roofs, broken windows, and flooding.

If your bathroom was damaged by a hurricane or tropical storm, a proper restoration goes far beyond surface-level repairs. Here is what you need to know about rebuilding your bathroom the right way.

Assessing Water Damage

Water damage in a bathroom after a hurricane can be deceptive. What looks like minor staining on walls or ceilings often hides serious problems behind the drywall. Standing water, even for a short period, can saturate subfloors, warp framing, and create the perfect environment for mold growth.

A professional assessment should include checking the subfloor for soft spots or warping, inspecting behind walls for moisture using a moisture meter, evaluating plumbing connections that may have shifted or cracked during the storm, and checking electrical wiring and outlets for water exposure. Skipping this step can lead to costly problems down the road, including structural failure and health hazards from hidden mold.

Mold Remediation

Mold is one of the most serious consequences of hurricane damage in Louisiana. The combination of heat, humidity, and standing water creates ideal conditions for mold to take hold within 24 to 48 hours. In bathrooms, mold commonly grows behind tile, under vanities, inside wall cavities, and beneath flooring.

Proper mold remediation requires containment of the affected area, removal of contaminated drywall, insulation, and flooring, treatment of framing and subfloor with antimicrobial solutions, and verification through post-remediation testing before reconstruction begins. This is not a do-it-yourself project. Professional mold remediation ensures the problem is fully resolved and your rebuilt bathroom starts with a clean, safe foundation.

Working with Insurance

Navigating insurance claims after a hurricane can be overwhelming. Many homeowners in Calcasieu Parish have dealt with delayed claims, underpayments, and disputes over the scope of covered work. Working with a contractor who understands the insurance process makes a significant difference.

At Lake Charles Bathroom Remodeling, we document every step of the demolition and rebuild process with detailed photographs and written records. This documentation supports your claim and helps ensure your insurance company covers the full scope of necessary work. We also provide itemized estimates that align with standard insurance adjustment formats, making the process smoother for you and your adjuster.

The Full Rebuild Process

A complete bathroom rebuild after hurricane damage typically follows these steps:

  • Demolition — Full tear-out of damaged drywall, tile, flooring, vanity, and fixtures. Damaged plumbing and electrical are removed as well.
  • Structural repair — Replacement of damaged framing, subfloor, and any compromised structural elements.
  • Mold remediation — Professional treatment and clearance testing before closing up walls.
  • Plumbing and electrical — New rough-in or repair of existing systems to meet current Louisiana building codes.
  • Waterproofing — Moisture barriers, mold-resistant drywall, and proper ventilation to protect against future water damage.
  • Finishing — Tile installation, vanity and countertop, fixtures, lighting, paint, and final trim work.
  • Inspection — Final walkthrough and any required inspections by the City of Lake Charles or Calcasieu Parish.

Building Back Better

A hurricane damage remodel is an opportunity to upgrade your bathroom with materials and techniques that better withstand the Gulf Coast environment. We recommend moisture-resistant drywall in all wet areas, porcelain or ceramic tile that resists water absorption, improved ventilation fans rated for humid climates, water-resistant luxury vinyl plank flooring as an alternative to traditional materials, and updated plumbing with corrosion-resistant fittings.

These upgrades not only protect your investment but also add value to your home and reduce the risk of damage in future storms.
