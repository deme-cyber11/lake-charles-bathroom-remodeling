Tub to Shower Conversion in Lake Charles, LA

Upgrade your bathroom with a modern walk-in shower tailored for Calcasieu Parish homeowners.

One of the most popular bathroom upgrades in Lake Charles is converting an old bathtub into a walk-in shower. Whether your tub is outdated, rarely used, or no longer practical for your household, a tub-to-shower conversion can transform the look and functionality of your bathroom. Here is everything you need to know about the process, from benefits and costs to timelines and accessibility options.

Why Convert Your Tub to a Shower?

Many homeowners in Calcasieu Parish are choosing to replace their bathtubs with walk-in showers for several compelling reasons:

  • More usable space — A walk-in shower can make a small bathroom feel larger and more open, especially with a frameless glass enclosure.
  • Easier daily use — Showers are faster and more convenient for daily routines. Most families find they rarely use their bathtub once children are past toddler age.
  • Improved safety — Stepping over a high tub wall is a fall risk, particularly for older adults. A low-threshold or curbless shower entry is significantly safer.
  • Modern appearance — Walk-in showers with custom tile, glass doors, and built-in niches give bathrooms a clean, contemporary look that appeals to buyers.
  • Better water management — A properly built shower with modern drainage handles water more efficiently, which matters in Louisiana's humid climate where moisture control is critical.

The Conversion Process

A professional tub-to-shower conversion in Lake Charles typically follows a straightforward process:

  • Consultation and measurement — We visit your home to evaluate the existing plumbing, measure the space, and discuss your design preferences. This is also when we identify any potential issues with the subfloor or drain placement.
  • Demolition — The old bathtub is removed along with the surrounding tile or tub surround. The subfloor is inspected for water damage or mold, which is common in older Lake Charles homes.
  • Plumbing modification — The drain is repositioned if needed, and the shower valve and supply lines are adjusted for the new configuration. A mixing valve upgrade is recommended if your home has older plumbing.
  • Waterproofing — The shower pan or base is installed, and the walls are waterproofed with a membrane system before any tile goes up. This step is essential for preventing moisture problems in the Gulf Coast climate.
  • Tile and finishing — Wall tile, floor tile, glass enclosure, fixtures, and accessories are installed. This includes any built-in niches, benches, or grab bars you selected during the design phase.
  • Final inspection — We complete a thorough walkthrough, test all plumbing, and ensure everything meets code requirements for the City of Lake Charles and Calcasieu Parish.

Accessibility Options

A tub-to-shower conversion is one of the best ways to improve bathroom accessibility for aging-in-place or mobility needs. Popular accessibility features include:

  • Curbless or zero-threshold entry — Eliminates the step into the shower entirely, allowing wheelchair or walker access.
  • Built-in shower bench — Provides a safe, comfortable seat for showering without standing.
  • Grab bars — Strategically placed grab bars near the entry, shower controls, and bench add stability and prevent falls.
  • Handheld showerhead — An adjustable-height handheld showerhead on a slide bar accommodates users of all heights and abilities.
  • Non-slip flooring — Textured tile or slip-resistant coatings keep the shower floor safe when wet.

Cost and Timeline

A standard tub-to-shower conversion in Lake Charles typically costs between $6,000 and $14,000 depending on the materials and features you choose. A basic conversion with a prefabricated shower base and tile surround falls at the lower end, while a fully custom tile shower with frameless glass, multiple showerheads, and a built-in bench will be at the higher end of the range.

Most conversions take one to two weeks from demolition to completion. The timeline depends on the complexity of the plumbing work and whether any subfloor or structural repairs are needed. Custom glass enclosures may add a few days for measurement and fabrication.

Considerations for Lake Charles Homeowners

If your home was affected by Hurricane Laura, Hurricane Delta, or any recent flooding, a tub-to-shower conversion is an opportunity to address hidden water damage at the same time. Many older tubs in Calcasieu Parish homes were installed decades ago, and the areas beneath and behind them often harbor moisture issues that only become visible during demolition.

We also recommend upgrading to mold-resistant drywall and proper ventilation during any shower conversion in the Lake Charles area. Louisiana's humidity levels demand materials and installation techniques that go beyond what works in drier climates.
